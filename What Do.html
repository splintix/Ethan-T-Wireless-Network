What does a wireless network do?

A wireless network does the same job as a wired network but their differences matter. A wireless network provides internet to a designated area without having to connect cables or wires to devices to provide internet access. A wireless network can be accessed by using a password given by an internet provider instead of using wires or cables. A wireless network broadcasts a signal in which computers or devices can detect and join. Wireless networks make life easier for people as there would not be any connection problems if someone moved around. Wireless networks are proven to be more popular than wired networks as the wireless networks is less troubling and is less costly to install.
